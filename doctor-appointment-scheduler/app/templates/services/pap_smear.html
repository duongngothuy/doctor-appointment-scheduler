Understanding Pap Smear Testing

What is a Pap Smear?

A Pap smear (or Pap test) collects cells from your cervix to check for abnormal changes that could develop into cervical cancer.

  • Simple, quick procedure
  • Detects abnormal cells early
  • Can prevent cervical cancer

HPV Testing

Often done alongside Pap smears, HPV testing checks for the virus that causes most cervical cancers.

  • Same sample, additional information
  • Identifies high-risk HPV types
  • Helps determine follow-up care

Screening Guidelines

Ages 21-29
Pap Smear Every 3 Years
  • Start at age 21
  • Pap test only (no HPV test)
  • Continue every 3 years
Ages 30-65
Multiple Options
  • Pap + HPV test every 5 years
  • OR Pap test every 3 years
  • OR HPV test every 5 years
Ages 65+

You may stop screening if you've had regular normal results and no history of serious cervical abnormalities. Discuss with your doctor.

What to Expect During Your Test

1
Before Your Appointment

Schedule for a time when you're not menstruating. Avoid douching, vaginal medications, or intercourse 24 hours before.

2
During the Exam

You'll lie on an exam table. A speculum is inserted to view the cervix, and cells are gently collected with a brush. Takes about 5 minutes.

3
After the Test

You can resume normal activities immediately. Results typically available in 1-2 weeks. We'll contact you with results and any follow-up needs.

Understanding Your Results

Normal Results
  • No abnormal cells found
  • Continue regular screening schedule
  • Next test in 3-5 years
Abnormal Results
  • Doesn't mean cancer
  • May need follow-up testing
  • Could indicate infection or minor changes

How to Prepare

Do
  • Schedule when not menstruating
  • Empty your bladder before the exam
  • Wear comfortable clothing
  • Bring questions to ask
Avoid 24 Hours Before
  • Douching
  • Vaginal medications
  • Sexual intercourse
  • Spermicidal products
