Types of Breast Cancer Screening

Digital Mammography

Advanced digital X-ray examination of the breast that can detect tumors before they can be felt during a clinical exam.

  • Takes 10-15 minutes
  • Minimal discomfort
  • High-resolution images

Clinical Breast Exam

Physical examination performed by a healthcare professional to check for lumps or other changes in breast tissue.

  • Part of routine checkup
  • Non-invasive examination
  • Complements mammography

Breast Ultrasound

Uses sound waves to create images of breast tissue. Often used as a follow-up to mammography for further evaluation.

  • No radiation exposure
  • Painless procedure
  • Excellent for dense breast tissue

Screening Guidelines

What to Expect During Your Visit

1
Before Your Appointment

Schedule for the week after your period when breasts are less tender. Avoid deodorant, perfume, or powder on your chest area.

2
During the Exam

You'll undress from the waist up and put on a hospital gown. A technologist will position you for the X-ray images.

3
After Your Screening

Results are typically available within 1-2 weeks. You'll receive a detailed report, and we'll contact you if any follow-up is needed.
