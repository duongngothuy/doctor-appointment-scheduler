Understanding HPV

What is HPV?

Human papillomavirus (HPV) is a common sexually transmitted infection. Most sexually active people will get HPV at some point in their lives.

  • Over 200 types of HPV exist
  • Some types cause cancer
  • Often no symptoms present

HPV Vaccine Protection

The HPV vaccine protects against the types of HPV that most commonly cause cancer and genital warts.

  • Prevents cervical cancer
  • Protects against genital warts
  • Reduces vaginal and vulvar cancers

Vaccination Schedule

Ages 9-14
2-Dose Series
  • First dose: Any time
  • Second dose: 6-12 months later
  • Most effective at this age
Ages 15-26
3-Dose Series
  • First dose: Any time
  • Second dose: 1-2 months later
  • Third dose: 6 months after first
Ages 27-45

Talk to your doctor about whether HPV vaccination is right for you. Some adults who are not already protected might decide to get vaccinated after speaking with their doctor.

What to Expect

1
Before Vaccination

Brief consultation to review medical history and discuss any questions. No special preparation needed.

2
During Vaccination

Quick injection in the upper arm. Takes less than a minute. Most people experience only mild discomfort.

3
After Vaccination

15-minute observation period. You'll receive a vaccination record card. Schedule your next dose if needed.

Side Effects

Common (Mild)
  • Arm soreness
  • Redness at injection site
  • Mild swelling
  • Low-grade fever
When to Call
  • High fever (>101°F)
  • Severe allergic reaction
  • Persistent pain
  • Any concerns
